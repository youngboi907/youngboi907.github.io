Understanding the Greeks

Delta (Δ)

Range: 0 to 1 (for calls), -1 to 0 (for puts)
Meaning: Expected price change for $1 move in underlying
Use: Gauge directional exposure & probability ITM

Gamma (Γ)

Range: 0 to 1
Meaning: Rate of change of delta
Use: High gamma = fast delta changes (risky around expiry)

Vega (ν)

Range: Typically 0 to 0.50
Meaning: Price change for 1% change in IV
Use: Profit from volatility expansions/contractions

Theta (Θ)

Range: Negative for long calls/puts, Positive for shorts
Meaning: Daily profit/loss from time decay
Use: Premium sellers benefit; long options lose to decay

Implied Volatility (IV)

Range: % annualized
Meaning: Market's expectation of future volatility
Use: High IV = expensive premiums; Low IV = cheap premiums

Rho (ρ)

Range: Typically small
Meaning: Price change per 1% change in interest rate
Use: Less relevant for crypto; mainly for longer-dated options
